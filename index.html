Questions

What people ask before switching.

01
What is Making Tax Digital for Income Tax?
MTD for ITSA is HMRC's replacement for the annual Self Assessment return. Instead of one return a year, you keep digital records and send HMRC four quarterly updates plus a Final Declaration. It begins in April 2026 for sole traders and landlords with qualifying income above £50,000.
02
Do I still need an accountant?
If you're a sole trader or landlord with no VAT registration and no employees, TaxLiteMTD covers the whole MTD obligation on its own. You may still want an accountant for more complex affairs, but for straightforward income the app is designed to be all you need.
03
Does this replace my Self Assessment?
Yes. Once you're in MTD for ITSA, the quarterly updates and Final Declaration take the place of the old SA100 return. You no longer file a separate annual Self Assessment for that income.
04
When do the rules apply to me?
From April 2026 for qualifying income over £50,000; from April 2027 for income over £30,000; and from April 2028 for income over £20,000 (proposed). The thresholds step down each year, so most sole traders and landlords will be brought in over time.
05
Is TaxLiteMTD recognised by HMRC?
TaxLiteMTD is in active development and going through HMRC's recognition process for MTD-compatible software. We'll update this page the moment that recognition is confirmed.
06
How is my data handled?
Your records are held on your device by default and are never removed without your say-so. The connection to HMRC uses their official OAuth flow, so your Government Gateway credentials are never stored by us. Premium adds encrypted cloud backup you control.
