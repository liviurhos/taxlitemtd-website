FAQ

Frequently asked questions

What is Making Tax Digital for Income Tax (MTD for ITSA)?
MTD for ITSA is HMRC's new system that replaces the annual Self Assessment tax return. From April 2026, self-employed people and landlords with income over £50,000 must submit quarterly updates to HMRC digitally, followed by a Final Declaration at the end of the tax year.
Do I still need an accountant if I use TaxLiteMTD?
For most self-employed people and landlords without VAT registration or employees, TaxLiteMTD replaces the need for an accountant entirely. You submit your quarterly updates and Final Declaration directly to HMRC through the app.
Does TaxLiteMTD replace Self Assessment?
Yes. Once enrolled in MTD for ITSA, your quarterly updates plus Final Declaration replace the traditional SA100 Self Assessment tax return. You no longer need to file a separate Self Assessment.
Who needs to use MTD for ITSA?
From April 2026: self-employed people and landlords with gross income over £50,000/year. From April 2027: those with income over £30,000. From April 2028: those with income over £20,000 (proposed).
Is TaxLiteMTD approved by HMRC?
TaxLiteMTD is currently in development and undergoing HMRC's approval process to become recognised MTD-compatible software. We will update this page as soon as we receive full approval.
Is the subscription tax deductible?
Yes. The cost of TaxLiteMTD is a legitimate business expense and can be deducted from your taxable profit.
What happens to my data if I cancel?
Your data is stored locally on your device and is never deleted without your consent. Premium cloud data can be exported as PDF before cancelling.
